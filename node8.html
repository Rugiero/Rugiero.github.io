March – Signal Propagation in a City

People in the city trying to contact their fellow mates by cell phones will have to exchange data by electromagnetic waves with their base stations. This data is relevant only to the parties communicating with each other. As some of the data signals gets leaked to the surroundings, each transmission will cause an interference field, which will disturb other transmissions in the same frequency band.

Interference in big crowds gets so large that data transmission rates will lower to the level of jamming. This can be seen for example in crowded festival areas, when calling to a friend is impossible.

Figure: Interference field developing in time. Complex surrounding is modeled as random multi path signal propagation from random locations moving to random directions in time. Also, a line-of-sight component is assumed. Red occurrences will cause remarkable disturbance in communication.
